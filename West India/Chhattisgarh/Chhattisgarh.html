About Chhattisgarh

Known as the "Rice Bowl of India", Chhattisgarh is blessed with stunning waterfalls, dense forests, ancient temples, and vibrant tribal culture.

The Soul of Central India

Chhattisgarh, carved out of Madhya Pradesh in 2000, is one of India's youngest states with one of the oldest cultures. The state is home to over 42 different tribes, each with their unique traditions, art forms, and festivals.

The landscape is dotted with waterfalls, wildlife sanctuaries, and ancient monuments that tell stories of bygone eras. From the majestic Chitrakote Falls to the mystical temples of Sirpur, Chhattisgarh offers a perfect blend of nature, adventure, and spirituality.

With over 44% of its area under forests, Chhattisgarh is a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. The state's rich mineral resources have earned it the nickname "India's Power Hub".

Culture & Heritage

Discover the vibrant traditions and rich heritage of Chhattisgarh

Rich Tribal Heritage

Chhattisgarh is home to over 42 different tribes, each with their unique customs, traditions, and way of life. The major tribes include Gond, Baiga, Bison Horn Maria, Abhuj Maria, Muria, Halba, and Dhurvaa.

The tribal communities have preserved their ancient traditions, rituals, and art forms despite modernization. Their close connection with nature is reflected in their worship of natural elements and forest deities.

Traditional tribal dances like the Panthi, Raut Nacha, Karma, and Saila are performed during festivals and special occasions, showcasing their vibrant culture.

Exquisite Art & Craft

Chhattisgarh is renowned for its traditional handicrafts that reflect the skill and creativity of its artisans. The state is particularly famous for its bell metal craft, wrought iron work, terracotta items, and bamboo products.

The tribal art of Chhattisgarh includes beautiful paintings, wood carvings, and metal figurines. The Dhokra art (metal casting using the lost-wax technique) is particularly noteworthy and has gained international recognition.

Other notable crafts include Kosa silk weaving, stone carving, and tribal jewelry making using beads, cowries, and metals.

Vibrant Festivals

Chhattisgarh celebrates a mix of traditional tribal festivals along with major Hindu festivals. The Bastar Dussehra is the most famous, lasting for 75 days and involving the entire tribal community in unique rituals.

Other important festivals include Madai Festival, Goncha Festival, Hareli (celebrating agriculture), and Pola (bull worship festival). These festivals showcase colorful costumes, traditional music, and dance performances.

The state also hosts several cultural events and fairs throughout the year, providing a platform for local artists and craftsmen to display their talents.

Learn More

Unique Cuisine

Chhattisgarhi cuisine is simple yet flavorful, with rice being the staple food. The state is known as the "Rice Bowl of India" and offers a variety of rice-based dishes.

Popular dishes include Chila (rice pancakes), Faraa (rice dumplings), Aamat (bamboo shoot curry), and Bafauri (steamed lentil dumplings). The tribal communities have their unique culinary traditions using local ingredients.

Traditional beverages include Landa (rice beer) and Sulphi (extracted from palm trees). Sweets like Dehrori and Khurmi are popular during festivals.
