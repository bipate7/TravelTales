The Last Paradise of India

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a breathtaking archipelago of 572 emerald islands, islets, and rocks in the Bay of Bengal. With only about 38 islands inhabited, this union territory offers untouched natural beauty, some of the world's most spectacular coral reefs, and a fascinating blend of cultures.

From the historic Cellular Jail in Port Blair that echoes India's freedom struggle to the pristine Radhanagar Beach consistently ranked among Asia's best, every corner of these islands tells a unique story. The islands are home to indigenous tribes like the Jarawa and Sentinelese, some of the last uncontacted peoples on Earth, adding to the archipelago's mystique.

Island Chronicles

1789

The British establish their first colony in the Andamans, naming it Port Cornwallis (later Port Blair).

1858

The British build the Cellular Jail, known as "Kala Pani," to exile Indian freedom fighters.

1942-1945

Japanese occupation during World War II. Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose raises the Indian flag here.

1956

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands become a union territory of independent India.

2004

The islands are severely affected by the Indian Ocean tsunami, with significant ecological changes.

Present

A thriving tourism destination while protecting indigenous tribes and fragile ecosystems.

Island Treasures

Port Blair

Best: October to May

The capital city blends history with island charm. Visit the Cellular Jail's moving light and sound show, explore the marine life at Chidiya Tapu, and relax at Corbyn's Cove beach.

Havelock Island

Best: November to April

Home to Radhanagar Beach (Asia's best beach), Elephant Beach for snorkeling, and Kalapathar Beach for stunning sunsets. A paradise for divers and beach lovers alike.

Explore More

Neil Island

Best: November to April

A peaceful retreat with Bharatpur Beach's coral reefs, Laxmanpur Beach's sunset views, and the natural rock bridge at Howrah Bridge. Perfect for a relaxed island experience.

Explore More

Baratang Island

Best: December to March

Adventure through dense mangrove creeks to reach the stunning limestone caves. Witness the unique mud volcanoes and experience the indigenous Jarawa culture (from a distance).

Explore More

Ross and Smith Islands

Best: November to April

Twin islands connected by a stunning natural sandbar that appears during low tide. Perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and witnessing the unique marine ecosystem.

Explore More

Jolly Buoy Island

Best: December to April

Part of Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park, this island offers the best snorkeling with vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life including clownfish and sea turtles.

Island Flavors

Seafood Platter

Must Try Local Specialty

Fresh catch of the day including lobster, crab, prawns, and fish prepared with local spices and coconut. The signature dish is the Chilli Crab - blue swimmer crabs stir-fried in a sweet, spicy and savory sauce.

Best places to try: New Lighthouse Restaurant (Port Blair), Full Moon Cafe (Havelock)

Andaman Fish Curry

Traditional Spicy

Traditional preparation with fresh fish, coconut milk, tamarind, and aromatic spices. The Surmai Curry (king mackerel) is particularly famous. Served with steamed rice and fried plantains for a complete island meal.

Best places to try: Annapurna Restaurant (Port Blair), Fat Martin's (Havelock)

Tropical Fruits

Healthy Seasonal

Exotic fruits like mangosteen, rambutan, starfruit, and the rare Andaman Padauk fruit that grow abundantly on the islands. Don't miss the coconut water straight from the shell, available at every beach.

Best places to try: Local markets in Port Blair, fruit stalls near Radhanagar Beach
