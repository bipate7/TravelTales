About Bihar

Bihar, located in eastern India, is a land of ancient heritage and spirituality. It was the center of power, learning, and culture in ancient and classical India, home to great empires like the Maurya and Gupta dynasties.

The state is the birthplace of Buddhism, with Bodh Gaya being the most important pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. It's also the origin of Jainism and has played a significant role in the development of Indian civilization.

Today, Bihar is a fascinating blend of ancient traditions and modern development, offering visitors a unique glimpse into India's glorious past and vibrant present.

Bihar's Culinary Delights

Litti Chokha

Bihar's signature dish – wheat balls stuffed with sattu, served with mashed vegetables.

Sattu Paratha

Nutritious flatbread stuffed with roasted gram flour – a Bihari specialty.

Khaja

Flaky, layered sweet pastry – a specialty of Silao near Nalanda.

Thekua

Traditional sweet snack made from wheat flour and jaggery during Chhath Puja.

Chana Ghugni

Spicy chickpea curry served as a popular street food across Bihar.

Malpua

Sweet pancake dessert, especially relished during Holi festival.

Major Festivals of Bihar

Chhath Puja

October/November

The most important festival of Bihar dedicated to the Sun God, celebrated with fasting and offerings at riverbanks.

Sonepur Mela

November/December

Asia's largest cattle fair held at the confluence of Ganges and Gandak rivers.

Buddha Purnima

April/May

Celebration of Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and death, especially grand in Bodh Gaya.

Rajgir Mahotsav

October

Cultural festival showcasing dance, music, and arts from Bihar and beyond.
