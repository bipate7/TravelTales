About East India

East India is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and historical monuments. It includes the states of Odisha, Bihar, Jharkhand, West Bengal, and the Union Territory of Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

East India, a region rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, offers an unparalleled travel experience. From the vibrant cities to the serene beaches and lush forests, this region is a treasure trove of wonders waiting to be explored.

Majestic Mountains and Serene Landscapes

Embark on an adventure to the breathtaking landscapes of Odisha and Jharkhand. Witness the serene beaches of Andaman and Nicobar Islands, tranquil lakes, and lush valleys that offer a perfect escape for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Historical Marvels and Architectural Splendors

Delve into the historical richness of East India with visits to iconic landmarks such as the Sun Temple in Konark, the ancient city of Pataliputra in Bihar, and the Victoria Memorial in Kolkata. Each monument tells a story of a bygone era, reflecting the grandeur of India's royal past.

Spiritual Sanctuaries and Sacred Sites

East India is a spiritual haven with an abundance of revered temples and pilgrimage sites. Explore the holy city of Puri, home to the famous Jagannath Temple, and experience the spiritual energy that draws millions of pilgrims and seekers from around the world.

Vibrant Culture and Festivals

Immerse yourself in the vibrant cultural tapestry of East India, where traditional music, dance, and art forms come alive. Join in the festivities of Durga Puja, Chhath Puja, and other colorful celebrations that showcase the region's rich cultural heritage.

Exquisite Cuisine and Culinary Delights

Savor the diverse and delectable cuisine of East India, from the mouthwatering sweets of Bengal to the spicy curries of Odisha. Each dish is a culinary masterpiece, reflecting the region's unique flavors and culinary traditions.

Thriving Wildlife and Nature Reserves

For wildlife enthusiasts, East India offers thrilling experiences in its national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. Encounter majestic elephants, tigers, and exotic bird species in the Sundarbans, Simlipal National Park, and Betla National Park.

Warm Hospitality and Welcoming Locals

One of the highlights of traveling in East India is the warm hospitality of its people. Experience the genuine warmth and friendliness of the locals, who will make your journey even more memorable with their hospitality and stories of the region's rich heritage.

Places to Travel in East India

Konark Sun Temple, Odisha

Konark Sun Temple

Nalanda University, Bihar

Nalanda University

Visit the ancient ruins of Nalanda University, one of the oldest universities in the world and a center of learning in ancient India.

Victoria Memorial

Marvel at the grandeur of the Victoria Memorial, a symbol of Kolkata's rich colonial history and architectural splendor.

Sundarbans, West Bengal

Sundarbans

Explore the Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world and home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger.

Havelock Island, Andaman

Havelock Island

Relax on the pristine beaches of Havelock Island, known for its crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and water sports activities.

Cuisine of East India

Rasgulla, West Bengal

A famous Bengali sweet made from chhena (Indian cottage cheese) and soaked in syrup.

Litti Chokha, Bihar

A traditional dish consisting of dough balls stuffed with roasted gram flour and served with mashed vegetables.

Dalma, Odisha

A wholesome dish made with lentils and a variety of vegetables, seasoned with traditional spices.

Dhuska, Jharkhand

A popular dish made from rice and lentil batter, deep-fried to perfection.

Seafood, Andaman

Experience a variety of fresh seafood dishes, including fish, crabs, and prawns, straight from the Andaman waters.

Festivals of East India

Durga Puja, West Bengal

A grand celebration dedicated to Goddess Durga, marked by elaborate decorations and cultural performances.

Chhath Puja, Bihar

A significant festival dedicated to the Sun God, celebrated with great devotion and rituals.

Rath Yatra, Odisha

A major festival where deities are taken out in a grand procession, attracting millions of devotees.

Karma Festival, Jharkhand

A tribal festival celebrating the harvest season, with traditional music, dance, and rituals.

Island Tourism Festival, Andaman

An annual festival showcasing the culture and traditions of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, with various events and activities.
