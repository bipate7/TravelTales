Top 10 Things to Do in Jaipur

From visiting historic palaces to enjoying traditional Rajasthani cuisine, find out the top 10 things you must do when in Jaipur.

Last Updated: August 11, 2023 | Author: TravelTales Team

Discover the Pink City's Hidden Gems

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is a vibrant city that beautifully blends tradition with modernity. Known as the Pink City for its trademark building color, Jaipur is a treasure trove of history, culture, and unforgettable experiences. Here are our top 10 recommendations for your Jaipur itinerary:

  1. Explore the Amber Fort

    This majestic fort-palace complex is Jaipur's crown jewel. Don't miss the Mirror Palace (Sheesh Mahal) and the stunning views from the fort walls.

  2. Visit the Hawa Mahal

    The iconic "Palace of Winds" with its 953 windows was built to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals while remaining unseen.

  3. Wander Through City Palace

    This sprawling complex includes the Chandra Mahal and Mubarak Mahal palaces and a museum with an impressive collection of royal costumes.

  4. Marvel at Jantar Mantar

    This UNESCO World Heritage Site features the world's largest stone sundial among its collection of 19 astronomical instruments.

  5. Shop at Johari Bazaar

    Jaipur's famous jewelry market is perfect for buying traditional Kundan and Meenakari jewelry, gemstones, and handicrafts.

  6. Enjoy a Traditional Thali

    Savor authentic Rajasthani cuisine at places like Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB) or Suvarna Mahal at Rambagh Palace.

  7. Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride

    See Jaipur from above with a sunrise balloon ride offering breathtaking views of the city's landmarks.

  8. Visit Nahargarh Fort at Sunset

    This hilltop fort offers panoramic views of the city, especially magical during sunset hours.

  9. Experience a Bollywood Movie at Raj Mandir

    Watch a film at India's most famous cinema, known for its opulent art deco interiors.

  10. Attend a Cultural Evening at Chokhi Dhani

    This ethnic village resort offers a complete Rajasthani experience with folk dances, camel rides, and traditional food.

Planning Your Jaipur Adventure

Jaipur is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between October and March when the weather is pleasant. The city is well-connected by air, rail, and road, making it easily accessible from major Indian cities.

For a truly immersive experience, consider staying in one of Jaipur's heritage hotels that were once palaces or havelis (mansions) of the nobility. Many offer royal treatment at surprisingly affordable prices.
