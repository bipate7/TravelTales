About North India

North India is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and historical monuments. It includes the states of Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Uttarakhand, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, and Rajasthan.

North India, a region rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, offers an unparalleled travel experience. From the majestic Himalayan peaks to the sacred rivers and bustling cities, this region is a treasure trove of wonders waiting to be explored.

North Indian Cuisine

Discover the rich and diverse culinary traditions of North India, known for its aromatic spices and hearty dishes that will tantalize your taste buds.

Punjabi Cuisine

Butter Chicken, Sarson ka Saag

Punjabi Cuisine

Known for its rich, buttery dishes and tandoori cooking style. Must-try dishes include Butter Chicken, Dal Makhani, and Makki di Roti with Sarson ka Saag.

Best places to try: Amritsar, Chandigarh

Food Tours

Rajasthani Cuisine

Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi

Rajasthani Cuisine

Characterized by its use of gram flour, lentils, and minimal water. Famous dishes include Dal Baati Churma, Laal Maas, and Ker Sangri.

Best places to try: Jaipur, Udaipur

Food Tours

Kashmiri Cuisine

Rogan Josh, Gushtaba

Kashmiri Cuisine

Known for its aromatic dishes using saffron, dry fruits, and yogurt. Must-try dishes include Rogan Josh, Yakhni, and Kashmiri Pulao.

Best places to try: Srinagar, Gulmarg

Food Tours

Street Food

Chaat, Parathas, Kebabs

Street Food

North India is famous for its vibrant street food culture. Don't miss Delhi's Chaat, Amritsari Kulcha, Lucknowi Kebabs, and Banarasi Paan.

Best places to try: Delhi, Lucknow

Cultural Timeline

Journey through the rich historical tapestry of North India's civilizations and empires.

Indus Valley Civilization

3300–1300 BCE

One of the world's earliest urban civilizations with advanced city planning and architecture.

Maurya Empire

322–185 BCE

Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, with Ashoka promoting Buddhism across Asia.

Gupta Empire

320–550 CE

Golden Age of India with advancements in science, mathematics, and arts.

Delhi Sultanate

1206–1526

Established Islamic rule in North India, leaving behind impressive architecture.

Mughal Empire

1526–1857

Created iconic monuments like the Taj Mahal and Red Fort.

British Raj

1858–1947

Colonial period that shaped modern India's infrastructure and institutions.
