Tour Overview

The Golden Triangle tour is India's most popular tourist circuit, connecting the capital city Delhi with Agra (home of the Taj Mahal) and Jaipur (the Pink City) in Rajasthan. This journey through North India offers a perfect introduction to the country's rich history, diverse cultures, and architectural wonders.

Detailed Itinerary

1

Day 1: Arrival in Delhi - The Capital Experience

Your journey begins in Delhi, where our representative will greet you at the airport. After settling into your hotel, embark on a guided tour of Old Delhi, visiting:

  • Red Fort: The magnificent 17th-century Mughal fortress
  • Jama Masjid: India's largest mosque with capacity for 25,000 worshippers
  • Chandni Chowk: The bustling wholesale market dating back to the 17th century

Experience the contrast with New Delhi as you see:

  • India Gate: War memorial arch inspired by Paris's Arc de Triomphe
  • Rashtrapati Bhavan: The President's House with its 340 rooms
  • Humayun's Tomb: Precursor to the Taj Mahal and UNESCO World Heritage Site

Enjoy a welcome dinner with traditional dance performances at a heritage haveli.

Overnight: The Imperial Delhi or similar (5-star heritage property)

2

Day 2: Delhi to Agra - The Taj Mahal Awaits

Morning drive to Agra (approx. 3 hours via the Yamuna Expressway). After lunch, visit:

  • Taj Mahal at sunset: Witness the marble glow golden as the sun sets. Learn the romantic story behind Shah Jahan's tribute to his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Our guide will show you the perfect photography spots and explain the intricate pietra dura inlay work.
  • Agra Fort: This massive red sandstone fortress offers stunning views of the Taj from its balconies. Learn about Emperor Shah Jahan's imprisonment here by his son Aurangzeb.

Evening at leisure to explore local markets for marble handicrafts or enjoy spa services at your hotel.

Overnight: Trident Agra or similar (with views of the Taj Mahal)

3

Day 3: Agra to Jaipur via Fatehpur Sikri

Early morning optional visit to see the Taj Mahal at sunrise when the marble appears pink in the morning light (highly recommended). Then drive to Jaipur, stopping at:

  • Fatehpur Sikri: The abandoned Mughal city built by Emperor Akbar in 1571. Explore the stunning Jama Masjid, Buland Darwaza (54m high gateway), and the tomb of Sufi saint Salim Chishti.

Continue to Jaipur (approx. 4 hours), stopping for lunch at a countryside resort. Arrive in Jaipur and check into your heritage-style hotel.

Evening free to relax or explore the vibrant local bazaars for textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts.

Overnight: Alsisar Haveli or similar (heritage property)

4

Day 4: Jaipur - The Pink City

Full day exploring Jaipur:

  • Amber Fort: Ride elephants (or jeeps) up to this hilltop fort with its mirrored halls and stunning views. The fort's construction began in 1592 and showcases both Hindu and Mughal architectural styles.
  • Hawa Mahal: The iconic "Palace of Winds" with its 953 windows designed for royal ladies to observe street life unseen.
  • City Palace: The royal residence with museums displaying weapons, costumes, and manuscripts. See the world's largest silver vessels in the palace.
  • Jantar Mantar: The fascinating 18th-century astronomical observatory with massive instruments that still provide accurate predictions.

Enjoy a special Rajasthani thali dinner with folk music and dance performances at a heritage property.

Overnight: Alsisar Haveli or similar

5

Day 5: Jaipur to Delhi - Departure

Morning visit to a local artisan workshop to see:

  • Block printing: Watch artisans create intricate patterns using traditional wooden blocks
  • Gem cutting: Jaipur is famous for its gemstone industry - see how raw stones are transformed into jewels

Drive back to Delhi (approx. 5 hours) with lunch en route at a countryside resort. Transfer to airport for your onward journey, taking unforgettable memories of India's Golden Triangle.

Essential Information

The ideal time for the Golden Triangle tour is from October to March when temperatures are pleasant (typically 10°C to 25°C). December and January can be cooler, especially in the mornings and evenings, so pack some light warm layers. This is peak tourist season, so monuments will be busier but the weather makes for comfortable sightseeing.

Summers (April-June) can be extremely hot (up to 45°C), making afternoon sightseeing challenging. However, this is when you'll find fewer crowds and better hotel rates. If traveling in summer, we recommend starting sightseeing very early in the morning.

Monsoon season (July-September) brings occasional showers but also lush green landscapes and dramatic skies. The rain typically comes in short bursts, allowing for sightseeing between showers. This is the least crowded time to visit.

This tour involves moderate physical activity. You should be comfortable walking 3-5 miles per day, often on uneven surfaces at historical sites. Some locations like Fatehpur Sikri and Amber Fort involve climbing steps and walking on cobblestones.

The Amber Fort elephant ride is optional (you can choose to walk or take a jeep instead). The walk up to the fort is about 10-15 minutes uphill. At the Taj Mahal, you'll walk about 1km from the gate to the mausoleum.

If you have mobility concerns, please let us know - we can arrange for wheelchairs at some sites (available for rent at Taj Mahal and Red Fort) and adjust the itinerary where possible. Many hotels have elevators, but some heritage properties may only have stairs.

  • Clothing: Dress comfortably but modestly, especially when visiting religious sites (shoulders and knees should be covered). Light, breathable fabrics are ideal for most of the year. In winter (Dec-Jan), bring layers as mornings can be chilly.
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes with good support are essential. You'll be removing shoes at some religious sites, so slip-ons are convenient.
  • Sun protection: A hat, sunglasses and sunscreen are recommended year-round. The sun can be intense even in winter months.
  • Accessories: Many travelers find a scarf useful - for sun protection, covering your head in certain religious sites, and as an extra layer.
  • Electronics: Universal power adapter (India uses 230V, Type D and Type M plugs), extra camera batteries/memory cards.
  • Health items: Basic medications (pain relievers, stomach remedies), hand sanitizer, any prescriptions with copies.
  • Other: Small backpack for daily essentials, reusable water bottle (we provide bottled water but this helps reduce plastic), travel umbrella if visiting in monsoon.
