About Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh, India's most populous state, is a land where history whispers from ancient walls, spirituality flows in sacred rivers, and culture thrives in vibrant traditions.

The Cradle of Indian Civilization

Uttar Pradesh has been the epicenter of Indian culture for over 4000 years. Home to three UNESCO World Heritage Sites - the iconic Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and the archaeological remains at Sarnath - the state's history spans the ancient Vedic period through the great Maurya and Gupta empires to the magnificent Mughal dynasty.

From the spiritual ghats of Varanasi, the oldest living city in the world, to the architectural wonders of Lucknow's Nawabi era, from the sacred towns of Mathura-Vrindavan to the wildlife sanctuaries of Dudhwa, Uttar Pradesh offers an unparalleled diversity of experiences that capture the essence of India.

The state is also renowned for its exquisite traditional arts - the delicate Chikankari embroidery of Lucknow, the luxurious Banarasi silk sarees, the intricate brassware of Moradabad, and the exquisite marble inlay work of Agra that adorns the Taj Mahal.
