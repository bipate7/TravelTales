Welcome to Rajasthan

Rajasthan, the largest state in India, is a land of contrasts and colors. Known for its royal heritage, the state boasts some of the most magnificent forts, palaces, and temples in the country. The Thar Desert, vibrant festivals, and warm hospitality make Rajasthan a must-visit destination.

The state's history dates back to ancient times, with evidence of human settlement found in the Indus Valley Civilization. Rajasthan was ruled by various Rajput kingdoms, which built spectacular forts and palaces that stand as testaments to their glorious past.

Pushkar - Holy City

Central Rajasthan 4.5

Famous for its sacred lake and Brahma Temple, Pushkar is an important pilgrimage site and hosts the vibrant Pushkar Camel Fair.

Ranthambore National Park

Eastern Rajasthan 4.8

One of the best places in India to spot wild tigers, along with other wildlife species in their natural habitat.

Desert

Bikaner

Best: October to March 4.4

Known for its desert landscape and camel safaris, Bikaner is home to Junagarh Fort, Karni Mata Temple, and the Bikaner Camel Festival.

Hill Station

Mount Abu

Best: November to March 4.7

The only hill station in Rajasthan, Mount Abu is known for its cool climate and scenic beauty. Attractions include Dilwara Temples, Nakki Lake, and Guru Shikhar.

Explore More
Spiritual

Ajmer

Best: October to March 4.5

A historical and religious city, Ajmer is famous for the Ajmer Sharif Dargah. Other attractions include Ana Sagar Lake and Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra.
