About North India

North India is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and historical monuments. It includes the states of Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Uttarakhand, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, and Rajasthan.

North India, a region rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, offers an unparalleled travel experience. From the majestic Himalayan peaks to the sacred rivers and bustling cities, this region is a treasure trove of wonders waiting to be explored.

Majestic Mountains and Serene Landscapes

Embark on an adventure to the breathtaking landscapes of Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand. Witness the snow-capped Himalayas, tranquil lakes, and lush valleys that offer a perfect escape for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Historical Marvels and Architectural Splendors

Delve into the historical richness of North India with visits to iconic landmarks such as the Taj Mahal in Agra, the Red Fort in Delhi, and the ancient forts and palaces of Rajasthan. Each monument tells a story of a bygone era, reflecting the grandeur of India's royal past.

Spiritual Sanctuaries and Sacred Sites

North India is a spiritual haven with an abundance of revered temples, monasteries, and pilgrimage sites. Explore the holy cities of Varanasi and Haridwar, where the sacred Ganges River flows, and experience the spiritual energy that draws millions of pilgrims and seekers from around the world.

Vibrant Culture and Festivals

Immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of North India, characterized by its colorful festivals, traditional music, and dance. Participate in the lively celebrations of Diwali, Holi, and Navratri, and savor the rich culinary delights that vary from region to region.

Wildlife and Adventure

For the wildlife enthusiast, North India boasts several national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, such as Jim Corbett National Park and Ranthambore National Park, where you can encounter a diverse array of flora and fauna. Adventure seekers can indulge in activities like trekking, river rafting, and skiing in the mountainous regions.

Modern Marvels and Urban Adventures

Experience the blend of modernity and tradition in the bustling cities of Delhi, Jaipur, and Chandigarh. These urban centers offer a mix of historical sites, contemporary architecture, shopping hubs, and vibrant nightlife.

Places to Travel in North India

Taj Mahal

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic monuments in India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

How to go: Reach Agra by train, road, or air.

Shimla

Himachal Pradesh

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a popular hill station known for its colonial architecture, beautiful landscapes, and pleasant climate.

How to go: Reach Shimla by train, road, or air.

Manali

Himachal Pradesh

Manali is a popular hill station in Himachal Pradesh known for its scenic beauty, adventure sports, and as a gateway to Ladakh.

How to go: Reach Manali by road or air. The nearest airport is in Bhuntar.

Jaipur

Jaipur

Known as the Pink City, Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthan and home to stunning palaces, forts, and vibrant bazaars.

Varanasi

Varanasi

Situated on the banks of the Ganges River, Varanasi is one of the oldest living cities in the world and a major spiritual center in India.

North Indian Cuisine

Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken is a popular dish in North Indian cuisine, known for its creamy tomato-based gravy and tender chicken pieces.

Famous In: Punjab

Rogan Josh

Rogan Josh is a signature dish from Kashmir, made with tender lamb cooked in a rich, aromatic curry.

Famous In: Kashmir

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular street food from Delhi, consisting of spicy chickpeas (chole) served with fried bread (bhature).

Famous In: Delhi

Makki di Roti & Sarson da Saag

A traditional Punjabi dish made of maize flour bread (makki di roti) served with mustard greens (sarson da saag).

Famous In: Punjab

Dum Aloo

Dum Aloo is a popular dish in Kashmir, made with baby potatoes cooked in a rich, spicy yogurt-based gravy.

Famous In: Kashmir

Litti Chokha

Litti Chokha is a traditional dish from Bihar, consisting of wheat balls stuffed with sattu, served with mashed vegetables (chokha).

Famous In: Bihar

Festivals of North India

Diwali

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is celebrated across North India with grand festivities, fireworks, and the lighting of lamps.

Celebrated In: All North Indian States

Holi

Holi is the festival of colors, celebrated with vibrant hues, music, dance, and traditional sweets.

Celebrated In: All North Indian States

Navratri

Navratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to Goddess Durga, celebrated with dance, fasting, and prayers.

Celebrated In: Uttar Pradesh, Delhi, Himachal Pradesh

Baisakhi

Baisakhi is a harvest festival celebrated with great zeal in Punjab, marking the beginning of the harvest season.

Celebrated In: Punjab

Lohri

Lohri is a popular winter festival in Punjab, celebrated with bonfires, dance, and traditional foods.

Celebrated In: Punjab

Dussehra

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, celebrates the victory of good over evil and is marked by the burning of effigies of Ravana.

Celebrated In: Uttar Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Delhi

Handicrafts of North India

Pashmina Shawls

Pashmina shawls are luxurious and soft, handwoven from fine wool in Kashmir.

Origin: Kashmir

Phulkari Embroidery

Phulkari is a traditional embroidery art from Punjab, known for its intricate floral patterns.

Origin: Punjab

Chikankari

Chikankari is a delicate and artful embroidery style from Lucknow, featuring intricate patterns.

Origin: Uttar Pradesh

Brassware

Moradabad is famous for its brassware, including statues, lamps, and utensils.

Origin: Uttar Pradesh

Wood Carvings

Kashmiri wood carvings are known for their intricate details, often seen in furniture and decor items.

Origin: Kashmir

Bamboo Crafts

Bamboo crafts from Uttarakhand include baskets, furniture, and decorative items, showcasing local craftsmanship.

Origin: Uttarakhand
