Mizoram at a Glance

Northeast India

Capital: Aizawl

Highest Point: Phawngpui (2,157m)

Area: 21,081 sq km

Rich Cultural Heritage

Mizoram's culture is a vibrant tapestry of traditions, music, dance, and festivals that reflect the Mizo way of life. The people are known for their warm hospitality and strong community bonds.

The traditional Cheraw dance, performed with bamboo sticks, is a visual spectacle and has gained international recognition.

Music

Traditional instruments like the Khuang and Darbu create unique Mizo melodies

Textiles

Intricate handwoven patterns in vibrant colors adorn traditional Mizo attire

Cuisine

Bamboo shoots, fermented pork, and smoked meats define Mizo flavors

Religion

Predominantly Christian with beautifully harmonized church choirs

Mizo Cuisine

Discover the unique flavors of Mizoram's traditional dishes

Bai

A hearty stew made with vegetables, bamboo shoots, and pork or fish, flavored with local herbs.

Must Try

Vawksa Rep

Smoked pork cooked with bamboo shoots and local spices, a signature Mizo dish.

Non-Veg

Sawhchiar

Mizo-style rice porridge cooked with chicken or pork, often served for breakfast.

Comfort Food

Chhangban

Sticky rice cakes, a popular snack often served with tea or as festival food.

Vegetarian

Vibrant Festivals

Celebrate the colorful festivals of Mizoram

Chapchar Kut

March

Spring festival marking the end of winter, featuring traditional dances, music, and feasting.

Mim Kut

August-September

Maize festival honoring ancestors with offerings of the first harvest and cultural performances.

Pawl Kut

December-January

Harvest festival celebrating the end of the agricultural year with community feasts and dances.
