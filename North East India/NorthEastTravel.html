About Northeast India

Welcome to Northeast India, a land of rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and unique experiences. Discover the beauty and diversity of this enchanting region, known for its vibrant cultures and breathtaking vistas.

Northeast India is a mesmerizing part of the country, comprising eight distinctive states: Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, and Tripura. Often referred to as the "Seven Sisters" and their brother Sikkim, each state offers its own unique charm and attractions.

Immerse yourself in lush greenery, majestic mountains, serene valleys, and pristine rivers. Experience the mystical monasteries of Sikkim, the living root bridges of Meghalaya, the tea gardens of Assam, and the colorful festivals of Nagaland. Northeast India promises an array of unforgettable adventures, perfect for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and cultural explorers alike.

Despite its natural beauty and cultural richness, Northeast India remains one of the lesser-explored regions, preserving its untouched allure and tranquil environment. Traveling through this region offers a rare opportunity to delve into diverse traditions, languages, and lifestyles. Whether you're trekking through the Himalayas, cruising down the Brahmaputra River, or experiencing the vibrant local festivals, Northeast India is a destination that captivates and inspires at every turn.

Join us in exploring this hidden gem of India, where every journey is an adventure, and every moment is a discovery.

Places to Travel

Majuli

Majuli, the world's largest river island, is known for its scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage.

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its population of the endangered one-horned rhinoceros.

Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji, one of the wettest places on Earth, is known for its living root bridges and stunning waterfalls.

Tawang

Tawang, nestled in the Himalayas, is known for its ancient monasteries and breathtaking mountain views.

Gangtok

Gangtok, the capital of Sikkim, offers stunning views of the Himalayas, vibrant markets, and a peaceful environment.

Loktak Lake

Loktak Lake, the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, is known for its floating islands and scenic beauty.

Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake, also known as Barapani, is a picturesque man-made reservoir offering a tranquil retreat.

Cuisine of Northeast India

Assamese Thali, Assam

A wholesome meal featuring rice, fish curry, green leafy vegetables, and regional delicacies.

Bamboo Shoot, Nagaland

A popular Naga dish made with bamboo shoots, often prepared with pork or fish.

Eromba, Manipur

A traditional dish made with boiled vegetables, fermented fish, and fiery chilies.

Thukpa, Arunachal Pradesh

A hearty noodle soup with vegetables and meat, influenced by Tibetan cuisine.

Smoked Pork, Meghalaya

A delectable Khasi dish of smoked pork, cooked with bamboo shoots and spices.

Festivals of Northeast India

Bihu, Assam

A vibrant festival celebrating the Assamese New Year, marked by traditional music and dance.

Hornbill Festival, Nagaland

A grand celebration showcasing the culture and heritage of Nagaland's tribes.

Durga Puja, Tripura

A major festival honoring Goddess Durga, featuring elaborate rituals and festivities.

Ningol Chakouba, Manipur

A family-oriented festival where married women are invited to a feast at their parental homes.

Wangala Festival, Meghalaya

A harvest festival celebrated by the Garo tribe, with traditional dances and music.
