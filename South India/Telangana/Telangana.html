The Rich History of Telangana

Telangana, the youngest state in India, has a history that dates back to the ancient Satavahana dynasty (230 BCE to 220 CE). The region has been ruled by various dynasties including the Kakatiyas, the Qutb Shahis, and the Nizams, each leaving behind a unique cultural and architectural legacy.

Satavahana Era (230 BCE - 220 CE)

The Satavahanas established their kingdom with Pratishthana (modern Paithan) as their capital, contributing to the development of art, architecture, and trade.

Kakatiya Dynasty (1083–1323 CE)

The Kakatiyas built the iconic Warangal Fort and the Thousand Pillar Temple, establishing Telangana as a center of art and culture.

Qutb Shahi Period (1518–1687)

The Qutb Shahi dynasty founded Hyderabad and built architectural marvels like the Charminar and Golconda Fort.

Asaf Jahi Dynasty (1724–1948)

The Nizams ruled Hyderabad State, making it one of the richest princely states in India, known for its pearls, diamonds, and the famous Koh-i-Noor diamond.

Formation of Telangana State (2014)

After a long struggle, Telangana was officially formed as the 29th state of India on June 2, 2014, with Hyderabad as its capital.

Telangana's Rich Culture

Discover the vibrant traditions that make Telangana unique

Cuisine

From the world-famous Hyderabadi Biryani to Sarva Pindi and Jonna Rotte, Telangana's cuisine is a flavorful journey through spices and traditions.

Arts & Music

Experience the rhythmic beats of Dappu, the graceful movements of Perini dance, and the soulful melodies of Telangana's folk music traditions.

Festivals

Celebrate Bathukamma, Bonalu, and Sammakka Saralamma Jatara - festivals that showcase Telangana's spirit and agricultural heritage.

Handicrafts

Discover the intricate Bidriware metalwork, Pochampally ikat weaves, and Nirmal paintings that reflect the region's artistic heritage.

Architecture

From the Kakatiya's thousand-pillared temples to the Qutb Shahi's Charminar, Telangana's architecture tells stories of its glorious past.

Tribal Heritage

Explore the rich traditions of Telangana's tribal communities like the Lambadas, Gonds, and Koyas through their art, dance, and lifestyle.
